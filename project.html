About the Festival

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of the most significant festivals in Hindu culture. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. The festival usually lasts five days and includes various rituals, prayers, and celebrations.

During Diwali, families decorate their homes with diyas (oil lamps) and colorful rangolis (artistic patterns made with colored powders). Fireworks light up the night sky, and people share sweets and gifts with loved ones.

This festive season brings joy, prosperity, and togetherness, making it a special time for families and communities to come together.
